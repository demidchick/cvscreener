The Head of IT Operations is responsible for overseeing the technology infrastructure of an organization, managing IT projects, and ensuring the alignment of IT services with business goals. This role includes leading IT operational teams, budgeting for IT expenditures, and implementing effective IT processes that enhance service delivery across the organization. Based on current job market analysis and industry standards, successful Head of IT Operationss typically demonstrate:

  • Strategic Planning, IT Service Management (ITSM), Project Management, Budgeting and Financial Management, Risk Management, Vendor Management, Team Leadership, Change Management
  • 7-10 years in IT operations or related field with at least 3-5 years in a leadership role.
  • Strong Leadership Skills, Excellent Communication, Problem-Solving Mindset, Adaptability to Change, Strategic Thinking, Detail-Oriented

According to recent market data, the typical salary range for this position is $140,000 - $180,000, with High demand in the market.

Initial Screening Questions

Industry-standard screening questions used by hiring teams:

  • What attracted you to the Head of IT Operations role?
  • Walk me through your relevant experience in Information Technology / Technology Services.
  • What's your current notice period?
  • What are your salary expectations?
  • Are you actively interviewing elsewhere?

Technical Assessment Questions

These questions are compiled from technical interviews and hiring manager feedback:

  • How do you ensure service uptime and high availability for IT systems?
  • Can you describe a challenging IT project you managed and how you achieved its goals?
  • What ITSM tools have you used, and how have they improved service delivery?
  • How do you approach budgeting for IT operations?
  • What experience do you have with cloud computing and virtualized environments?
Expert hiring managers look for:
  • Ability to demonstrate knowledge of ITIL practices
  • Experience with managing IT budgets
  • Understanding of cloud services and infrastructure management
  • Ability to explain risk management strategies and tools
Common pitfalls:
  • Being unprepared to discuss specific IT operational challenges
  • Failing to provide examples from previous experience
  • Not demonstrating understanding of current technology trends
  • Inability to convey the importance of user experience in operations

Behavioral Questions

Based on research and expert interviews, these behavioral questions are most effective:

  • Describe a time when you had to implement a significant change in IT operations. How did you manage it?
  • How do you prioritize conflicting IT demands from various business units?
  • Can you give an example of how you built a successful IT team culture?
  • Discuss a time you faced a major IT failure. What did you learn from the experience?

This comprehensive guide to Head of IT Operations interview questions reflects current industry standards and hiring practices. While every organization has its unique hiring process, these questions and evaluation criteria serve as a robust framework for both hiring teams and candidates.
