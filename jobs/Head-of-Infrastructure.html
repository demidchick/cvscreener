The Head of Infrastructure is responsible for overseeing and managing the infrastructure that supports an organization's IT service delivery, including server management, network operations, data storage, and cloud services. This role involves strategic planning, budgeting, and leadership of the infrastructure team to ensure optimal performance and reliability of IT systems. Based on current job market analysis and industry standards, successful Head of Infrastructures typically demonstrate:

  • Cloud Computing, Network Architecture, Project Management, Cybersecurity, Disaster Recovery Planning, Vendor Management
  • 10+ years in IT infrastructure roles with at least 5 years in a management position, preferably in a similar industry.
  • Strong leadership and communication skills, Analytical thinking, Problem-solving abilities, Strategic vision, Ability to work under pressure

According to recent market data, the typical salary range for this position is $150,000 - $250,000, with High demand in the market.

Initial Screening Questions

Industry-standard screening questions used by hiring teams:

  • What attracted you to the Head of Infrastructure role?
  • Walk me through your relevant experience in Information Technology, Telecommunications, Financial Services.
  • What's your current notice period?
  • What are your salary expectations?
  • Are you actively interviewing elsewhere?

Technical Assessment Questions

These questions are compiled from technical interviews and hiring manager feedback:

  • What experience do you have with cloud infrastructure?
  • How would you assess the performance and scalability of our current infrastructure?
  • Can you explain the steps you would take to improve network security?
  • What tools and technologies do you use for monitoring the infrastructure?
  • How do you approach disaster recovery planning?
Expert hiring managers look for:
  • Understanding of network topology
  • Experience with cloud services (AWS, Azure, GCP)
  • Knowledge of cybersecurity best practices
  • Ability to develop and present infrastructure strategies
  • Hands-on experience with CI/CD processes
Common pitfalls:
  • Inability to explain past experiences clearly
  • Lack of technical depth in infrastructure technologies
  • Not staying updated with recent technological developments
  • Failing to demonstrate strategic thinking
  • Neglecting to mention team management experience

Behavioral Questions

Based on research and expert interviews, these behavioral questions are most effective:

  • Describe a time when you successfully led a major infrastructure project.
  • How do you handle conflicts within your team?
  • Tell us about a time you had to make a difficult decision regarding infrastructure investments.
  • How do you prioritize tasks in a high-pressure environment?
  • What steps do you take to maintain team morale and motivation?

This comprehensive guide to Head of Infrastructure interview questions reflects current industry standards and hiring practices. While every organization has its unique hiring process, these questions and evaluation criteria serve as a robust framework for both hiring teams and candidates.
