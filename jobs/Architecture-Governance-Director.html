The Architecture Governance Director is responsible for establishing and enforcing architectural standards and policies across hybrid technology environments. This role involves leading architecture governance processes, ensuring compliance with enterprise architecture frameworks, and collaborating with cross-functional teams to align IT strategy with business objectives. Based on current job market analysis and industry standards, successful Architecture Governance Directors typically demonstrate:

  • Enterprise Architecture, Governance Frameworks, Stakeholder Management, Risk Management, Compliance and Standards, Strategic Planning, Leadership and Influence
  • 10+ years of experience in IT architecture, governance, or related fields, including management roles overseeing architecture programs.
  • Strategic Thinker, Strong Communicator, Collaborative Leader, Detail-Oriented, Problem Solver, Mentor and Coach

According to recent market data, the typical salary range for this position is $140,000 - $180,000, with High demand in the market.

Initial Screening Questions

Industry-standard screening questions used by hiring teams:

  • What attracted you to the Architecture Governance Director role?
  • Walk me through your relevant experience in Information Technology.
  • What's your current notice period?
  • What are your salary expectations?
  • Are you actively interviewing elsewhere?

Technical Assessment Questions

These questions are compiled from technical interviews and hiring manager feedback:

  • What frameworks do you use for enterprise architecture governance?
  • How do you assess compliance with architectural standards?
  • Can you describe a challenging architecture governance issue you've faced and how you resolved it?
  • How do you ensure alignment between IT strategy and business objectives?
Expert hiring managers look for:
  • Knowledge of TOGAF, Zachman Framework or similar methodologies
  • Experience in developing governance policies
  • Ability to articulate the business value of architecture decisions
  • Proficiency in risk assessment and management practices
Common pitfalls:
  • Failing to demonstrate understanding of governance frameworks
  • Not providing specific examples of past governance challenges
  • Ignoring the importance of stakeholder engagement in governance processes
  • Underestimating the communication skills required for this role

Behavioral Questions

Based on research and expert interviews, these behavioral questions are most effective:

  • Describe a time when you had to enforce a governance policy that was met with resistance. How did you handle it?
  • Can you give an example of a successful architecture initiative you led? What was your role?
  • How do you prioritize initiatives within your governance framework?
  • Tell me about a time you had to influence senior stakeholders around a technical decision. What approach did you take?
