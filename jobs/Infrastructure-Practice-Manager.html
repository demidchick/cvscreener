The Infrastructure Practice Manager oversees the development and implementation of infrastructure solutions within an organization. This role focuses on managing a team that designs, installs, and maintains technology infrastructure to ensure it aligns with the strategic goals of the organization. The manager is responsible for ensuring high performance, reliability, and security of all infrastructure components, while also being an advocate for innovation and efficiency in processes. Based on current job market analysis and industry standards, successful Infrastructure Practice Managers typically demonstrate:

  • Project Management, Cloud Technologies (AWS, Azure, GCP), Network Management, Team Leadership, Budget Management, Systems Integration, Security Compliance, Infrastructure Automation
  • 10+ years in IT infrastructure management with at least 5 years in a leadership role.
  • Strong Leadership Skills, Excellent Communication Skills, Strategic Thinking, Problem Solving, Adaptability, Client-Focus Mindset

According to recent market data, the typical salary range for this position is $120,000 - $160,000, with High demand in the market.

Initial Screening Questions

Industry-standard screening questions used by hiring teams:

  • What attracted you to the Infrastructure Practice Manager role?
  • Walk me through your relevant experience in Information Technology / Telecommunications.
  • What's your current notice period?
  • What are your salary expectations?
  • Are you actively interviewing elsewhere?

Technical Assessment Questions

These questions are compiled from technical interviews and hiring manager feedback:

  • What is your experience with cloud infrastructure management?
  • Can you explain the difference between IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS?
  • How would you approach designing a secure network infrastructure?
  • What tools and frameworks do you use for monitoring infrastructure performance?
Expert hiring managers look for:
  • Knowledge of current infrastructure technologies
  • Ability to articulate infrastructure design and implementation strategies
  • Understanding of ITIL/DevOps practices
  • Experience with capacity planning and resource management
Common pitfalls:
  • Failing to demonstrate hands-on experience with current technologies
  • Being unable to provide examples of past project successes or failures
  • Lack of understanding in industry standards and best practices
  • Overlooking the importance of security in infrastructure design

Behavioral Questions

Based on research and expert interviews, these behavioral questions are most effective:

  • Describe a time you managed a challenging team project. What was your approach?
  • How do you handle conflicts within your team?
  • Can you give an example of how you successfully implemented change in your organization?
  • What strategy do you use to keep your team motivated and productive?

This comprehensive guide to Infrastructure Practice Manager interview questions reflects current industry standards and hiring practices. While every organization has its unique hiring process, these questions and evaluation criteria serve as a robust framework for both hiring teams and candidates.
