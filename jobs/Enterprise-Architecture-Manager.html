The Enterprise Architecture Manager is responsible for overseeing the enterprise architecture strategy and framework to ensure that technology aligns with the business strategy. This role involves leading a team of architects, evaluating new technologies, and ensuring that architecture principles are adhered to throughout the organization. The manager must work closely with stakeholders across the organization to understand business needs and how technology can support them, while also ensuring compliance with best practices and governance standards. Based on current job market analysis and industry standards, successful Enterprise Architecture Managers typically demonstrate:

  • Strategic Planning, Stakeholder Management, Cloud Computing, IT Governance, Framework Development (TOGAF, Zachman), Business Process Optimization, Communication Skills
  • 7+ years in IT roles with at least 3-5 years in an enterprise architecture position, preferably with managerial experience.
  • Analytical Thinking, Problem-solving Skills, Leadership, Flexibility and Adaptability, Excellent Communication Skills

According to recent market data, the typical salary range for this position is $120,000 - $180,000, with High demand in the market.

Initial Screening Questions

Industry-standard screening questions used by hiring teams:

  • What attracted you to the Enterprise Architecture Manager role?
  • Walk me through your relevant experience in Information Technology.
  • What's your current notice period?
  • What are your salary expectations?
  • Are you actively interviewing elsewhere?

Technical Assessment Questions

These questions are compiled from technical interviews and hiring manager feedback:

  • What frameworks have you used in enterprise architecture, and how have you applied them?
  • Can you describe a time when you had to make a trade-off between business needs and technical feasibility?
  • How do you approach integrating new technologies into an existing architecture?
  • What tools do you use for architecture modeling, and why?
Expert hiring managers look for:
  • Understanding of enterprise architecture frameworks
  • Ability to align technology with business goals
  • Knowledge of emerging technologies and their implications
  • Experience in governance and compliance related to architecture
Common pitfalls:
  • Focusing too heavily on technical details without relating to business outcomes
  • Not demonstrating understanding of architecture's role in business strategy
  • Failing to provide specific examples from past experiences
  • Neglecting to address stakeholder needs

Behavioral Questions

Based on research and expert interviews, these behavioral questions are most effective:

  • Describe a challenging project you led and how you guided your team through it.
  • Can you provide an example of a time you had to influence decision-makers to adopt a new technology?
  • How do you prioritize competing demands from different business units?
  • Share a time when you failed to meet a project goal. What did you learn from it?

This comprehensive guide to Enterprise Architecture Manager interview questions reflects current industry standards and hiring practices. While every organization has its unique hiring process, these questions and evaluation criteria serve as a robust framework for both hiring teams and candidates.
